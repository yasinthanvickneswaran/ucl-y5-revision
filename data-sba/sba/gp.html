A 61-year-old woman attends her GP with a 3-month history of increasing thirst, polyuria, fatigue and a gradual 4 kg weight loss. Over the same period, she has noticed reduced appetite and intermittent generalised muscular aches with dull lower back pain. She has a BMI of 31 kg/m² and a strong family history of type 2 diabetes. Her medical history includes hypertension and hyperlipidaemia, treated with amlodipine and atorvastatin. Blood tests show an HbA1c of 89 mmol/mol. What is the most appropriate next step in management?
  • A. Initiate metformin monotherapy
  • B. Initiate metformin with an SGLT2 inhibitor
  • C. Initiate triple therapy with metformin, an SGLT2 inhibitor and a GLP-1 agonist
  • D. Refer for structured diabetes education
  • E. Arrange urgent CT imaging of the abdomen
Explanation: New onset diabetes in a 60+ year patient with weight loss is a RED FLAG and should exclude any pancreatic lesions.
